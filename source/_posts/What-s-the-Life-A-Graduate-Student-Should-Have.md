Writing product, having meetings with company, managing servers, handling tasks of teaching assistant, taking courses, reading papers, preparing demonstrations... As a graduate student in NCU, I have so many tasks and works to complete, just like an octupus. Focusing on only one task is impossible. Working days are usually not enough. I have to spent a lot of holidays and weekends on dealing with the tasks that have not been done yet. I have little time to study and research techniques / knowledge I'm interested in, or sharpen my skills of programming. Is this really a valuable life of graduate student? Does it really worth it?
<!-- more -->
My answer is: Maybe, but with suspicions.

## Graduate Life
As a graduate student of my Lab in NCU, as me, there's no chance to have a respite because I have to handle lots of tasks parallelly described above. There're the descriptions of what I'm actually resonsible of in detail...

Our lab maintains a GitLab server for managing our codes. If there's a problem with the GitLab server I'm responsible of, I need to jump in, seeking for possible problems, travelling in the server, and solve the problem in the end.

We have projects. Some of them have cooperation with company or National Research Unit while some of them doesn't. For me, I own two projects that have cooperation with company. As a result, I have deadlines. I need to try my best to meet it.
In one of the two projects I'm resopnsible of, I act as a busboy. Every members have their own main clear tasks, and so am I at first. But after I completed my first main task, if there're tasks that are not in their scope, these tasks belong to me. From the time I joined this project to now, my boss recuited some university students for help. However, it means the cost of communication is risen. I took almost all code reviews and basic tests of the merge-request from the recruited university students. At the same time, I have my own deadlines to meet.
My another project is actually what I love. More precisely, I like all of the project I'm responsible of but the second one, a real time web application, is my true love~ In the second project, I'm the only one person in charge. I go to Taipei for meetings every week, I surveyed possible and appropriate techniques for this project to success, I gave these techniques some tries, had comparisons, and had only 10 days implementation for the prototype with the real time mointoring ability all by my own.

Professor has course to teach. Therefore, we must take teaching assistant as our responsibility. In this course, I have a system to maintain. I have to give patches to this system. We pay one day each week to correct the quiz answers submitted by students. We revise examinations and have experimental implementations to test if our revision goes well. Also, we generate new test cases to test the correctness of students' program. One more thing is, I found there're errors in the quiz reference answers. It will lead to our wrong judgements to the students' quiz submitions. So I examined all of the quizzes in all chapters, record wrong reference answers, and discussed it with professor. This is really a time-consuming and tiring mission.

Don't forget, we are students. I have courses to take, have exams to pass, have homework or projects to be done. I paid all tuition by myself. So I really don't want to fail on any of the courses I've taken.

Furthermore, we had one demonstration of our project per week. For one demonstration, we need to spend a lot of time to prepare the environment and have rehearsals before demonstrations. It also eat out much time from me, also from my partner.

However, the truth is, act as an octupus makes people lose focus. Productivity decreases significantly when you are responsible for many many tasks simultaneously.
